I am Yiwei Shu, a Ph.D. student in robotics at the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences and the Technology and Engineering Center for Space Utilization, Chinese Academy of Sciences.

My research focuses on reliable autonomous robotic systems operating in uncertain and unstructured environments. I am particularly interested in field and planetary robotics, legged autonomous exploration, active SLAM, risk-aware motion planning, online recovery, and whole-arm manipulation.

I am currently developing autonomous exploration and evaluation systems for the Unitree Go2 quadruped robot using LiDAR, depth sensing, grid-based world models, and Isaac Lab lunar-terrain simulations. My related work covers calibrated world-model reliability, reactive manipulation, pose-constrained grasp planning, and low-cost visual-servo control for aerospace systems.

Before beginning my doctoral studies, I received a B.Eng. in Electronic Information Engineering from ShanghaiTech University in 2024 and studied as an exchange student at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign in 2023.

I am open to visiting Ph.D. opportunities and international research collaborations in field and planetary robotics.
